Sanitation, Hygiene and Water Protection

Sanitation

Proper sanitation prevents water contamination. Always use toilets and ensure sewage systems are working correctly. Avoid disposing of waste near rivers and lakes. Regular cleaning of household and community areas reduces the risk of waterborne diseases such as cholera or diarrhea.

Hygiene

Good hygiene directly protects water quality. Always wash your hands with soap before handling water or food, and after using the toilet. Use clean containers for storing drinking water to prevent contamination.

Maintaining personal and household hygiene reduces the spread of germs and ensures that water remains safe for drinking, cooking, and cleaning.

Water Protection

Protecting water sources is essential for safe drinking water. Do not throw garbage, chemicals, or human waste into rivers, lakes, or wells. Cover water containers at home and regularly clean storage tanks and wells.

Encourage community initiatives to conserve water, prevent pollution, and monitor water quality. These steps ensure long-term access to safe and clean water for everyone.
